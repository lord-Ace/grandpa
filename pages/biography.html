BIOGRAPHY OF LATE MR. JOSEPH IKEMEFUNE AZU

JOSEPH IKEMEFUNE AZU fondly called JOE-JOE was born on the 20th day of October, 1948 in Jos, Plateau State to Mr. Camillus Azu and Mrs. Regina Mgborie Azu both of blessed memory.

EDUCATION:

He started his primary school education in Ropp, Plateau State but completed it in Abuedo Primary School, Ubulu-uku, Delta State. He had his Secondary education in Yaba College of Technology, Lagos where he had and made his Grade 2 on the 28th day of April, 1969 and A Level- General Certificate of Education under the auspices of University of London in 1968. He furthered his education by first acquiring a Diploma Certificate in Basic Course in Computer Programming- the computer system used in IBM System 370 from the University of Lagos, computer department in 1974. He trained in ICL (International Computer Limited) Systems Engineering School and was certified a full member of the Computer Association of Nigeria in 1981. He trained as a SuperStar Installation Expert and became one on the 4th day of August 1982. Joe’s quest for education was unmatched; he graduated from the prestigious University of Lagos with a BSC (Hons) in Business Administration in 1985 and never stopped upgrading his skill in the computer world until his retirement.

WORKING EXPERIENCE:

As a young man determined to make it, he was employed as a science laboratory assistant in the University of Lagos while he was a student, he later was gainfully employed as a Trainee Programmer from 1975 -1977 at NCR (Nigeria) LTD and was later promoted to Analyst Programmer from 1977 – 1980. For greener pastures off course, he left NCR and worked with the ICL (Nig.) LTD. from 1980 -1987 where he first worked as a software support Executive then became the Systems Adviser. In 1987, he joined Kragha & Associates as a computer Analyst for Eight (8) years. Mr. Joe Azu was highly sort after in the computer world, he worked with System Management at Ogunlana Drive Lagos- State, and he finally joined the indigenous company Modern Business Machines (MBM) where he worked in Lagos, Abuja and Jos branches respectively until his retirement.

MEMORABLE MOMENTS:

Joe I. AZU became a full member of Computer Association of Nigeria (COAN) affiliated to International Federation for Information Process (IFIP) in 1981, he was a member of the Nigerian Institute of Management in 1985, he became a Fellow of COAN in 1993. As a Fellow of COAN he was nominated to train companies, industries and high ranked Army officers and government officials. During his stay in Plateau State, Jos, at the Night of Calibre, he was noted as having contributed to the development of Plateau State and was thereby recognized as “ A Calibre of the Plateau” under the auspices of Ministry of Commerce and Industry. The Ubulu-uku Development Union Jos Branch, appreciated and recognized his dedication and sacrifice while with them.

SPIRITUAL LIFE:

JOSEPH AZU was a confirmed and staunch Catholic, he worshipped at St. Dominic Catholic Church, Yaba Lagos, St, Monica Catholic Church, Rantya Jos, S.S Joachim & Anne Catholic Church, Ijegun, Ikotun and St. Michael Catholic Church Abuedo, Ubulu-uku. He was a member of the Association of the Sacred Heart of Jesus and Immaculate Heart of Mary and a member of the Catholic Men Organization of Nigeria (CMO) until his death.

SOCIAL LIFE:

JOE-JOE as he was fondly called loved to socialize. In his hay days, he was a member of the Ubulu-uku 82’ Club, and Ubulu-uku Minds Club. He was an active member of Ubulu-uku Development Union in all the branches of the places he ever lived. He headed the Umuanugwe and Umugbe meetings in Lagos until he left Lagos and upon his retirement to Ubulu-uku he joined the Akelekete Social Group and was a member until his demise. Joe-Joe loved being happy and never appreciated negativity or its vibes. He was known to stand and fight for truth and justice at all times.

SURVIVED BY:

Joseph Ikemefune Azu is survived by Wife, Children, Grand Children, Nieces, Nephews, Brothers, Sisters and In-laws and cousins. He was loved by many.
